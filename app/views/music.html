Half-Life

20:00 - 21:00

With members pulled from the four corners of Manchester….as long as the corners include Wilmslow and Biddulph (!).....Half-Life are a four strong Rock/Metal/Punk covers band guaranteed to get you singing and dancing (or trying to coordinate nodding your head and tapping your foot while holding a pint!) along with well-known tracks from a wide range of artists from late 60s Classic Rock to a bucket-load of more modern Noughties floor fillers. Between us, we’ve played across the UK and internationally, from Christenings (really!) to large festivals and are always ready to set up and rock any occasion large or small. With an ever expanding set list which currently includes tracks from artists such as Steppenwolf, Iron Maiden, Green Day, Red Hot Chilli Peppers, The Cult, Guns ‘n’ Roses, Queen and Bon Jovi, to pick just a few, book us and you’re guaranteed to hear plenty of songs which you’ll just have to stop what you’re doing and sing along to!

Paddle Faster

21:30 - 23:00

Having successfully encouraged you to get even more drunk on the various luscious festival beers of yesteryear, Paddle Faster are back with this super-reinforced, max-strength lineup to further entertain you, and again keep you good company whilst the beer flows! Paul Higham, Mat Walklate, India Shan Merrett, and Jonny Hulme promise to smash you round the lug 'oles by way of powerful guitar, fiddle, banjo, harmonica, flute and vocals, playing an upbeat mix of Celtic trad., Bluegrass, Blues, Americana, all topped with a healthy dash of the contemporary. These musical veterans have played together in a myriad of musical collaborations over the years, and with each originally emerging from distinctly different musical backgrounds, they combine to create a seriously hard-hitting sound and potent blend of unique acoustic styles. Get yer pints in early for this one..

Sleeping With The Fishes

20:00 - 21:00

Sleeping with the fishes have been working hard over the past years building a powerful repertoire of live and recorded songs. They broke into the festival circuit playing at Biker bashes and are now a regular fixture at festivals like Crosby in Liverpool and Wychwood in Cheltenham. This review from David Barnicle at ‘Good Vibrations magazine’ sums up what you can expect from them live.

Simply put, Sleeping with the Fishes are a marvelously cinematic, atmospheric and energy-filled live band. Such was the intensity from the off, the question, ‘Will this band ever come down?’ arose during song three. It did come down, eventually, however, in tempo only.. The intensity remained and for what they gave up in tempo, they more than made up for in depth, songmanship and emotion, which arguably results in a deeper impact on the listener. Sonically, its akin to Progs like Floyd and Genesis, having lunch with Arcade Fire and Feeder, while Billy Idol boozes.
